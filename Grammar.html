Grammar

Grammar is the set of structural rules governing the composition of sentences, phrases, and words in a language. It helps us understand how words and phrases are arranged to convey meaning and ensure clear communication.

Parts of Speech

In English grammar, words are categorized into different parts of speech based on their functions and roles in a sentence. The common parts of speech include nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, pronouns, prepositions, conjunctions, and interjections.

Sentence Structure

Sentences in English follow a specific structure that includes a subject, verb, and often an object. Understanding sentence structure helps us construct grammatically correct and meaningful sentences.

Tenses

Tenses are used to indicate the time of an action or event in English. There are three main tenses: past, present, and future. Each tense has different forms and is used in various contexts.

Verb Agreement

Subject-verb agreement refers to the matching of the subject and verb in a sentence. It ensures that the verb agrees with the subject in terms of number (singular or plural).
