Idiom

An idiom is a phrase or expression that has a figurative meaning different from its literal meaning. Idioms are commonly used in everyday language and add depth and color to the way we communicate.

Types of Idioms

Idioms can be categorized into different types, including:

  • Literal Idioms
  • Figurative Idioms
  • Idioms with Prepositions
  • Idioms with Body Parts
  • Idioms with Animals
  • Idioms with Colors
  • Idioms with Numbers

Usage of Idioms

Idioms are used to express a particular idea or convey a specific message in a concise and vivid way. They can be used in various contexts, such as in conversations, writing, and literature.

Examples of Idioms

Here are some examples of common idioms:

  • Break a leg - Good luck
  • Piece of cake - Something easy
  • Let the cat out of the bag - Reveal a secret
  • Kick the bucket - Die
  • Hit the nail on the head - Do or say something exactly right
  • Cost an arm and a leg - Very expensive
