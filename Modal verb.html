Modal Verb

Modal verbs, also known as modal auxiliaries, are a type of auxiliary verb that modify or change the meaning of the main verb in a sentence. They express various shades of meaning such as possibility, necessity, ability, permission, and more.

Common Modal Verbs

There are several common modal verbs in English, including:

  • Can
  • Could
  • May
  • Might
  • Must
  • Shall
  • Should
  • Will
  • Would

Usage of Modal Verbs

Modal verbs are used to express various meanings and functions in English sentences. Some common uses of modal verbs include:

  • Expressing ability: I can swim.
  • Indicating possibility: It may rain tomorrow.
  • Expressing permission: Can I borrow your pen?
  • Showing obligation: You must complete the assignment.
  • Expressing advice: You should see a doctor.
  • Indicating future actions: I will call you later.

Examples of Modal Verbs

Here are some examples of sentences using modal verbs:

  • She can speak three languages fluently.
  • We should arrive early for the meeting.
  • They might go to the concert tonight.
  • He must study for the exam tomorrow.
  • You may use my computer if you need it.
